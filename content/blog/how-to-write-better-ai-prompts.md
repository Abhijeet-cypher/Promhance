## Why Most People Get It Wrong

The biggest mistake people make is treating an AI like Google. When you type a vague query into a search engine (e.g., "blog post about AI"), Google does the heavy lifting of finding existing content. When you type that same query into an AI, it tries to generate something from scratch with zero context.

### The "Shot in the Dark" Approach

A typical bad prompt looks like this:
> *"Write a blog post about SEO."*

Why is this terrible?
1. **No Context:** Who is the target audience? Beginners? Experts?
2. **No Tone:** Should it be professional, casual, humorous, or academic?
3. **No Constraints:** Should it be 300 words or 2,000 words? Does it need specific **AI prompts for SEO**?

When you give an AI nothing to work with, it falls back on its most generic training data. The result is "fluff."

## The Formula: How to Write Better Prompts for AI

To stop getting generic fluff and start getting expert-level outputs, you need to master **crafting AI prompts** using the **CREATE** framework: Context, Request, Examples, Audience, Tone, and Extras (Constraints).

![Good vs Bad AI Prompt](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1759661990336-51bd4b951fea?q=80&w=2070&auto=format&fit=crop)
*A structured prompt always beats a vague, one-sentence request.*

### 1. Context is King (Contextual Prompts)
AI doesn't know who you are, what your business does, or what your goals are. You have to tell it. Before making your request, set the stage.
> **Instead of:** *"Write an email..."*
> **Try:** *"I am a SaaS founder writing to a warm lead who abandoned their checkout cart. Our product is a productivity tool for developers..."*

### 2. Be Hyper-Specific with Your Request
**Clear instructions** are vital. Tell the AI exactly what role it should adopt and what precise output you need.
> **Instead of:** *"Give me ChatGPT prompts."*
> **Try:** *"Act as a Senior SEO Strategist. Generate 10 specialized ChatGPT prompts for keyword research and content gap analysis."*

### 3. Provide Few-Shot Examples
If you want the output formatted a certain way, show the AI exactly what you mean. This is known as "few-shot prompting" and drastically increases accuracy.
> *"Here is an example of the writing style I want you to mimic: [Insert Example]. Now, write a new paragraph on..."*

### 4. Define Audience and Tone
An article written for a high school student will sound very different from an executive summary for a board of directors. Always define the tone (e.g., authoritative, witty, concise) and the target audience.

## 3 Advanced Techniques to Try Today

Once you have the basics down, elevate your game with these advanced **prompt engineering** techniques:

1. **Chain-of-Thought Prompting:** Ask the AI to "think step-by-step" before providing the final answer. This forces the model to reason through complex logic, drastically cutting down on hallucinations.
2. **Iterative Prompting:** Don’t expect perfection on the first try. Ask the AI to generate a draft, critique its own work based on a new set of rules, and rewrite it.
3. **Negative Constraints:** Tell the AI what *not* to do. (e.g., *"Do not use the words 'delve', 'testament', or 'landscape'. Avoid overly formal corporate jargon."*)
